Never start a company 50/50.
Adrian Barwicki · Founder · 10+ years building
01 / 07
I've seen more good companies die from this

than from running out of money.
02 / 07
When two founders own a company equally,

no one can decide anything
when they disagree.
03 / 07
Hiring the first sales lead.
Whether to raise.
Who fires whom.
Which market to pick.

All deadlocked.
04 / 07
The companies that survive a 50/50
quietly agree one founder is
"actually the CEO."

You already had the answer.
You just spread the cap table
for nothing.
05 / 07
One founder owns more than 50%.
Even by one share.

That person calls it
when the room can't agree.
Friendships survive that.

50/50 doesn't.
07 / 07
The other founder takes
a meaningful stake
plus a written role boundary.

You both understand the deal
on day one.
