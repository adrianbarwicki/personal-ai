The cheapest advisor is the one you pay in cash.
Adrian Barwicki · Founder
01 / 07
Advisors love equity.
It's free upside for them.

Founders give it
because it feels
like the polite thing to do.
02 / 07
Both are wrong.

1% of your company is
the most expensive line item
you'll ever sign.
03 / 07
1% of a $30M exit
= $300,000.

For three intro calls
and an occasional Slack reply.
04 / 07
That's an advisor
at a $300k/hour rate.

Most don't deliver it.
05 / 07
Pay advisors in cash.
Like you'd pay
any other consultant.

€5k a month for a real engagement.
€500 for a single intro call.
Pay people for work.

Give equity for risk.
07 / 07
Save your equity
for the people
taking actual risk with you.

Co-founders. Early hires.
Investors who unlock the round.
