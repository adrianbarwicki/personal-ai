Raising money is the easiest hard thing you'll ever do.
Adrian Barwicki · Founder
01 / 07
I've watched more good companies
die from a successful raise

than from a failed one.
02 / 07
The mechanism is always the same:

Round closes.
Founder feels validated.
Headcount doubles in 12 weeks.
03 / 07
Fundamentals haven't changed.

Burn doubled.
Runway halved.

Product trajectory
identical.
04 / 07
18 months later
you're back in the market.

Numbers don't support
the new valuation.
05 / 07
Money doesn't fix direction.

It compounds whatever
you were doing.

Including the wrong thing.
Closing the round
isn't the win.

The next 18 months are.
07 / 07
Founders who survive
a successful raise
are the boring ones.

They keep the team the same size
for 6 months. Then spend.
