# LinkedIn Post — The first hire is the only one that matters

**Pillar:** Founder/company building
**Goal:** Counter-intuitive take on hiring. High comment rate.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> Your fifth hire never recovers from a bad first hire.
>
> Founders obsess over the team. Hiring frameworks, interview loops, culture decks.
>
> None of that matters as much as one decision: who walked through the door first.
>
> Culture isn't what you put on a wall. It's what your second employee saw the first one do.
>
> – If your first hire works late and burns out — that's culture.
> – If your first hire pushes back on a bad founder decision and lives — that's culture.
> – If your first hire takes the credit for a team win — that's culture.
>
> Hires #2 through #20 take their cues from #1. Not from you. You're the founder. They expect you to behave the way you behave. The first hire is the data point that tells them what's normal here.
>
> Founders who get this wrong end up trying to "fix culture" at 30 people. There's no fixing it. You set it the day you wrote the offer letter to your first non-co-founder.
>
> Your first hire is the only one you can truly choose. The rest inherit the choice.
