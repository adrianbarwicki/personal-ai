## Post body (paste-ready)

```
Do you still pay your devs €100/h? You're doing it wrong.

A year ago, three specialists × 6 weeks × €100/h ≈ €72k per feature. Standard. Five handoffs from PM to QA. Nobody questioned it.

Today, the same feature costs me about €20k. One senior engineer, paired with AI, reading from a shared company-wide docs repo, ships it in two weeks. Same output. 70% less cost. Twice the speed.

But the cost isn't the interesting part. The interesting part is that the engineer's job changed.

They don't type the code anymore. They review what AI shipped, they coordinate the team, they decide what's good and what goes back for another pass.

Each of my companies now has one product-wide docs repo — engineering, design, bizdev, accounting — all in one place, readable by humans AND AI. The shared brain. PMs draft tickets in Jira using that context. AI reads the tickets and codes the task. The engineer reviews what comes back. Specialists handing things off to each other became the slow path.

The takeaway is simpler than the setup makes it sound:

You need fewer engineers.
More senior.
Paid way above market.

Saves money. Ships faster.

#AI #SoftwareDevelopment #Engineering #StartupLessons
```

## Why this works (structure)

1. **Hook** = unhinged-but-defensible cost claim. Above-the-fold guaranteed.
2. **Setup math** establishes the comparison is real, not vibes.
3. **The pivot** ("the change isn't AI made my engineers more productive") tees up the role inversion.
4. **3 concrete things you changed** = personal proof. PMs + AI + Jira reading is the copyable detail.
5. **The takeaway** is the screenshot moment — three short sentences end-stopped.
6. **"Saves money. Ships faster."** lands the why-do-this in five words.

No "Where am I wrong?" close. The takeaway speaks for itself; the comments will come from people who agree, disagree, or want specifics.

---

## Defending the numbers (prep for comments)

Two numbers will be questioned. Have answers ready:

**"70% less cost. 2× speed."**
Defensive answer: pick one feature shipped in 2025 (3-person team) vs one shipped in 2026 (solo + AI). Compare actual hours / cost / timeline. Round-number version is fine — be honest about which numbers are precise vs vibes.

**"Way above market" — what's the actual number?**
This will come up. Honest answer: depends on the engineer and the role, but the principle is: top decile of market rate, sometimes 1.5–2× the median for the role + location. The point isn't the multiple, it's the mindset — stop trying to save money on the IC. Save money by hiring fewer of them.
