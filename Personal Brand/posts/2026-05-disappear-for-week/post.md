# LinkedIn Post — I built systems so I could disappear for a week

**Pillar:** Lifestyle / discipline
**Goal:** Personality + the "automate to escape" framing that's core to the brand voice.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> I built the operations specifically so I could disappear for a week without my phone going off.
>
> Most operators are trapped by the business they built. If you have to be online to keep it running, you didn't build a company. You built a job.
>
> I wanted to train BJJ daily and climb mountains without checking Slack. That constraint forced everything:
>
> – Documented SOPs for every recurring task
> – AI handling guest communication 24/7
> – Daily reports the team runs themselves
> – A finance close that doesn't need me
>
> Not because I was lazy. Because the mountain doesn't wait.
>
> The best business decision I ever made was wanting to leave the country for a week.
>
> The decision forced me to find every dependency on me — and remove it.
>
> Most founders won't do this voluntarily. They wait until burnout forces them. By that point, the business is too dependent on them to disconnect cleanly. The fix is harder under stress.
>
> Plan the disappearance now. Let the constraint do the work.
