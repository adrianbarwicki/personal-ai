# LinkedIn Post — The 10x engineer is dead. The 10x team isn't.

**Pillar:** AI / engineering
**Goal:** Contrarian take on a beloved engineering trope. Strong comment-trigger.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> The mythical 10x engineer doesn't exist in 2026.
>
> The 10x team does.
>
> The 10x engineer was always a Bay Area myth. What actually existed was "one person, less coordination overhead." A solo engineer outshipped a team of five because there was no team to coordinate with.
>
> In 2026, that math inverted.
>
> One principled engineer with three agents in their toolchain outputs more than a team of ten without them. Not because the engineer is special. Because their coordination overhead is now near zero — they coordinate agents, not people.
>
> Agents don't have egos. They don't need 1:1s. They don't sandbag estimates. They don't quit. They show up at 3am and do exactly what you wrote down.
>
> The new bottleneck isn't talent. It's tooling literacy.
>
> The engineers compounding fastest right now treat their agent stack the way the best engineers used to treat their IDE — obsessively customised, deeply understood, completely personal.
>
> Hire the engineer who treats their tooling like a teammate.

## Alternative hook

> The best engineer on your team in 2026 isn't the one who writes the most code. It's the one who runs the most agents.
