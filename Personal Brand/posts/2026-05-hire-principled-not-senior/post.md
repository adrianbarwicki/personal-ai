# LinkedIn Post — Stop hiring senior engineers. Hire principled ones.

**Pillar:** AI / engineering
**Goal:** Provocative hiring take, defensible authority. Strong save rate from CTOs and founders.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> I stopped hiring senior engineers two years ago. Best decision I made.
>
> "Senior" used to mean: they've seen a lot of code. They've debugged hard things. They have pattern recognition you can't fake.
>
> That mattered when typing was the bottleneck. It isn't anymore.
>
> The hire that compounds in 2026 isn't the one with 15 years on a CV. It's the one who can:
>
> – Write down what an agent should build, precisely enough that the agent can do it
> – Recognise when the output is wrong, fast enough that they don't ship it
> – Ship without ego, when the agent did 90% of the work
>
> That's a principled engineer. They might be 24. They might not be on any "senior" track at their last job.
>
> They're the only ones whose output is still growing month over month.
>
> Years of experience is the wrong x-axis now. Principles is the right one.

---

## Alternative hook

> The best engineer on my team has 3 years of experience. Two years ago I would've called him a junior.
