# LinkedIn Post — Documentation, not operations

**Pillar:** Operator truth (Phase 1 — RENTUJEMY only)
**Goal:** Reframe a familiar founder pain. Save-worthy.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> If your team asks you the same question twice this week, you don't have an ops problem.
>
> You have a documentation problem.
>
> Most operators think they're drowning because the business is hard. It's not. They're drowning because no two people in the company do the same task the same way.
>
> The fix isn't more management. It's writing things down. Once. Properly.
>
> We run 300 properties with a documented SOP for every recurring task. The team doesn't ask me anything. They check the doc.
>
> When something breaks, we don't blame the person. We blame the doc. And we fix the doc.
>
> The operators who scale write things down. The ones who don't are stuck answering the same Slack message at 11pm for the rest of their career.
>
> Documentation is the cheapest scaling tool that exists. And the one most operators refuse to invest in.
