# LinkedIn Post — Most "AI features" in 2026 are bullshit

**Pillar:** AI / engineering
**Goal:** Authority + spicy take from someone actually shipping AI. High comment rate.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> 90% of the "AI-powered" product launches I see in 2026 are GPT wrappers with marketing.
>
> I build AI products. I know the tells.
>
> Three quick ones:
>
> 1. The demo never fails. They only show happy paths. Real AI products have ugly failure modes — they have to design for them. If a vendor never shows you a failure mode, that *is* the failure mode.
>
> 2. Latency on real workloads is 8 seconds, not the 0.8 you saw on stage. Anything cached or pre-recorded is suspect.
>
> 3. When the model is wrong, there's no fallback. Just a silent miss. Real systems have logging on every prompt, a manual override path, and different models routed for different intents. Wrappers have a single prompt and a hope.
>
> Real AI products are mostly safety nets, fallbacks, and observability. The model is the easy part.
>
> If their product looks like ChatGPT in a sidebar, it probably is.

## Alternative hook

> The fastest way to tell if "AI-powered" is real or marketing: ask to see it fail.
