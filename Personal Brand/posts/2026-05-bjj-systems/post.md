## Final copy (paste-ready)

> BJJ teaches you more about running a business than any MBA program.
>
> MBA teaches strategy. BJJ teaches what happens when strategy meets resistance.
>
> A few things I've learned on the mat that translate directly to running a 300-property operation:
>
> – The bigger person on top of you doesn't care about your plan. (Markets don't either.)
> – Position beats submission attempts every time. (Fix the system before you fix the bug.)
> – You lose to fundamentals long before you lose to skill. (Same with operations.)
> – The training partner who taps you out smiling is the one you learn from. (Same with co-founders.)
>
> Everything I know about scaling came from getting tapped out by people half my size in a gym in Warsaw.
>
> Position before submission. That's the whole game.
