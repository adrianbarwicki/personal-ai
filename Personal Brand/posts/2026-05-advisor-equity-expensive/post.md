# LinkedIn Post — Advisor equity is the most expensive line on your cap table

**Pillar:** Founder/company building
**Goal:** Polarising founder advice. Strong comment-trigger.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> The cheapest advisor is the one you pay in cash.
>
> Advisors love equity. It's free upside for them. Founders give it because it feels like the polite thing to do.
>
> Both are wrong.
>
> 1% of your company is the most expensive line item you'll ever sign.
>
> Math: 1% of a $30M exit is $300,000. For what — three intro calls and an occasional Slack reply? You just paid an advisor at a $300k/hour rate.
>
> Pay your advisors in cash. Like you'd pay any other consultant. €5k a month for a real engagement, or €500 for a single intro call. They'll either accept it or admit they weren't bringing $300k of value.
>
> Save your equity for the people taking actual risk with you — co-founders, early employees, sometimes one or two investors who unlock the round.
>
> Pay people for work. Give equity for risk. They're not the same.
