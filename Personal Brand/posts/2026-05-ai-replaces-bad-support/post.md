# LinkedIn Post — AI didn't replace customer support. It replaced bad customer support.

**Pillar:** AI / engineering
**Goal:** Reframe "AI is taking jobs" debate with operator data. Strong save rate.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> AI didn't replace customer support. It replaced bad customer support.
>
> I run a 24/7 AI voice agent that handles roughly 80% of incoming guest calls across our properties. After two years of this — I'm more convinced than ever that human support didn't go anywhere.
>
> The 20% the AI escalates is the hard 20%:
>
> – Confused customer who didn't understand the FAQ
> – Angry guest who needs to feel heard, not redirected
> – Multi-party situation (guest + cleaner + neighbour)
> – Anything with a real consequence if you get it wrong
>
> AI compressed the volume of trivial support to near zero. It concentrated the complexity in the cases that actually needed a human all along.
>
> That's not replacement. That's separation of concerns.
>
> The agents I keep on the team in 2026 are smarter, better-paid, and handle higher-stakes work than the support team I had three years ago. They also get to sleep at night, because the AI takes the 3am call asking what the WiFi password is.
>
> AI doesn't end human work. It concentrates it.
