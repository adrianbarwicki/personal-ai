# LinkedIn Post — Never start a company 50/50

**Pillar:** Founder/lifestyle texture
**Audience:** Founders, investors, would-be entrepreneurs
**Goal:** Authority + saves/shares from people debating co-founder splits
**Format:** Text post (no carousel needed — hot take format performs best as text)

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> Never start a company 50/50.
>
> I've seen more good companies die from this than from running out of money.
>
> When two founders own the company equally, no one can decide anything when they disagree. Hiring the first sales lead. Whether to raise. Who fires whom. Which market to pick.
>
> Equal weight on every decision sounds fair. In practice it's a deadlock generator.
>
> The companies that survive a 50/50 do it by quietly agreeing one founder is "actually the CEO" — at which point you already had the answer and just spread the cap table for nothing.
>
> If you're starting now: one founder owns more than 50%. Even by one share. That person calls it when the room can't agree. The other founder takes a meaningful equity stake plus a written role boundary. You both understand the deal on day one.
>
> Friendships survive that. Companies survive that.
>
> 50/50 doesn't.

## Comment-priming follow-up (drop as first comment 5 min after post)

> Caveat I get asked about: yes, 51/49 is enough. The point isn't dominance, it's that one person has the deciding vote when consensus breaks. Most disputes never get to that vote — but the fact that it exists changes the negotiation.
