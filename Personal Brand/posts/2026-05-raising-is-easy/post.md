# LinkedIn Post — Raising is easy, spending is hard

**Pillar:** Founder/company building
**Goal:** Reframe a common founder milestone. Strong save-rate.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> Raising money is the easiest hard thing you'll ever do.
>
> I've watched more good companies die from a successful raise than from a failed one.
>
> The mechanism is always the same:
>
> – Round closes.
> – Founder feels validated.
> – Headcount doubles in 12 weeks.
> – Fundamentals haven't changed.
> – Burn doubled. Runway halved. Product trajectory identical.
>
> 18 months later, you're back in the market raising again — except now your numbers don't support the new valuation, and the founder who raised the previous round is no longer the same person who has to defend it.
>
> Money doesn't fix direction. It compounds whatever you were doing — including the wrong thing.
>
> The founders who survive a successful raise are the boring ones. They close the round. They keep the team the same size for 6 months. They make sure the next dollar earned by the company is worth more than the one before it.
>
> Then, and only then, they spend.

---

## Alternative hook

> Closing a round is not the win. Surviving the 18 months that follow it is.
