# LinkedIn Post — If you can't draw your business model on a napkin

**Pillar:** Founder/company building
**Goal:** Sharp test founders apply to themselves. High shareability.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> If your business model needs a deck to explain, it's a deck — not a business model.
>
> Real businesses fit on a napkin:
>
> – We charge X per Y.
> – Customer pays us this much.
> – Costs us this much.
> – Margin is this.
>
> That's it.
>
> The minute you need three slides, a flowchart, and a Mermaid diagram to explain the model — you've got something that sounds good in a meeting and falls apart in a spreadsheet.
>
> Test yourself. Hand someone a napkin and a pen. If they can't draw your business in 60 seconds, you don't have one yet.
>
> The companies I know that compound the longest all have boringly simple napkin math. The ones that don't are usually one round away from explaining the model differently to the next investor.
>
> Napkin first. Deck later. Never the other way around.

---

## Alternative hook

> The investors I respect ask one question first: "What does this cost you and what does the customer pay?" If you fumble that, the meeting is over.
