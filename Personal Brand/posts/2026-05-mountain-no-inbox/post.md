# LinkedIn Post — The mountain doesn't reschedule for your inbox

**Pillar:** Lifestyle / discipline
**Goal:** Story-led personality post. Closes with a strong founder-applicable rule.
**Format:** Text post (story-led — slightly longer than the others)

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> The mountain doesn't reschedule for your inbox.
>
> Three years ago I left for a 7-day climb in the Alps. No reception, no phone, no laptop. By the time I came back, I'd missed 1,400 emails, 87 Slack mentions, and one client crisis that resolved itself within hours.
>
> The business — 300 properties — ran fine without me.
>
> Not because I'm a great manager. I wasn't there to manage anything.
>
> Because I'd been forced to build a company that runs without me. I left specifically so I'd have to.
>
> Every founder should be required to take a week offline once a quarter. The first time you do it, the business will tell you immediately what's broken. And by 'broken' I mean: what depends on you that shouldn't.
>
> The list gets shorter every time. By year two you start wondering why the company needs you in the office at all. By year three you stop wondering.
>
> Forced disconnection forced me to actually fix the business.
>
> Schedule the disconnection. The repairs follow on their own.

## Alternative hook

> The single most expensive cost in my business was me being available. Disconnecting once a quarter is the cheapest way I've found to fix it.
