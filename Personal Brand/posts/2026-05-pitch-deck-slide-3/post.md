# LinkedIn Post — Why pitch decks fail at slide 3

**Pillar:** Founder/company building
**Goal:** Authority + saves from founders preparing decks.
**Format:** Text post

---

## Final copy (paste-ready)

> Investors give your deck 90 seconds. Slide 3 is where they lose interest.
>
> It's not the team slide. It's not the product. It's the market slide.
>
> The one with TAM = $50B in a circle inside a square inside a bigger circle.
>
> That slide tells a sophisticated investor that you've never thought hard about your actual buyer. It signals: "I read a deck template and I copied the format."
>
> The good version of slide 3 is small and specific.
>
> Bad:
> > Global vacation rental market = $87B by 2030.
>
> Good:
> > 14,000 STR operators are on Hostaway. Average spend on tooling: $2,400/year. We capture $X per operator. That's a $34M serviceable market today, growing at 18% as Hostaway expands.
>
> The first one is wallpaper. The second one is research.
>
> If your market slide makes the investor smarter, you've won. If it makes them yawn, you've already lost the room.

---

## Alternative hook

> I've watched 50+ decks this year. The market slide is where 80% of them die.
