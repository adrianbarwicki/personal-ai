## Original thought

I want to create full ai driven company

## Context

Adrian is exploring the idea of structuring RENTUJEMY and/or Enhanced Rentals around AI as a core operating principle — not just as a tooling layer, but as the backbone of how the company runs. This likely means replacing or heavily augmenting traditional headcount with AI agents handling operations (guest comms, owner reporting, pricing, cleaning coordination, accounting), with humans playing a supervisory or exception-handling role rather than an execution role.

The timing makes sense: the ceo-assistant workflow, the n8n automation stack, and the Claude-based tooling already in place suggest the infrastructure is being laid incrementally. This idea may be about naming that direction explicitly and committing to it as a design constraint going forward.

## Why this matters

If executed, an AI-driven operating model would allow the business to scale revenue-per-property and portfolio size without a proportional increase in headcount — a significant structural advantage in the STR management space. It would also differentiate RENTUJEMY as a tech-first operator, which matters for owner acquisition and potential SaaS positioning of Enhanced Rentals. The 6–12 month win looks like: a defined set of core workflows fully automated end-to-end, with a clear map of which human roles remain and why.

## Next steps to validate

- [ ] Audit existing workflows and categorise each as: fully automated, partially automated, or human-only — establish the baseline
- [ ] Define what "AI-driven" means operationally: is the goal zero FTEs, minimal FTEs, or AI-augmented team with a specific ratio?
- [ ] Identify the 2–3 highest-leverage workflows to automate next (likely: owner reporting, guest comms escalation, payout reconciliation)
- [ ] Talk to at least one founder running a comparable lean/AI-first operation to pressure-test the model
