# Intercompany Cost Recharge Policy
## Enhanced Rentals GmbH — Gamma Global Partners FZCO
### Personnel Cost Recharge: GCC Sales & Integration Representatives

---

**Policy Reference:** ER-IC-001
**Issuing Entity:** Enhanced Rentals GmbH
**Effective Date:** 01.03.2026
**Policy Owner:** CEO / Finance
**Review Frequency:** Annual or upon material change
**Status:** Active

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy formalizes the methodology and procedures for recharging personnel costs incurred by Gamma Global Partners FZCO ("Gamma") to Enhanced Rentals GmbH ("ER GmbH") in connection with sales representatives and account personnel employed or contracted by Gamma for ER GmbH's benefit in the GCC region.

The policy ensures that:
- All intercompany charges are calculated consistently and transparently.
- The pricing applied is arm's length and defensible under German transfer pricing law (§1 AStG, §90 AO) and OECD guidelines.
- Both parties maintain adequate documentation for tax, audit, and regulatory purposes.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all cost recharge transactions between ER GmbH and Gamma arising from:

- The employment or contracting of sales and account representatives by Gamma on behalf of ER GmbH.
- Any directly attributable overhead or administrative costs incurred by Gamma in relation to such representatives.

This policy does not apply to:
- Commissions or revenue-sharing arrangements (if separately agreed).
- Technology licensing or software subscription fees.
- Third-party pass-through costs that Gamma invoices to end customers directly.

---

## 3. Recharge Methodology

### 3.1 Cost Base

The cost base for each recharge cycle comprises all costs directly and reasonably attributable to each representative, including:

| Cost Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Base salary / contractor fee | Gross monthly remuneration paid to or contracted from the representative |
| Statutory contributions | Any UAE mandatory social security, pension, or end-of-service gratuity contributions |
| Benefits | Health insurance, visa fees, and other employment-related benefits |
| Overhead allocation | A reasonable share of Gamma's management, HR, administration, and office costs attributable to the ER GmbH engagement |

Costs must be documented with supporting records (payroll summaries, invoices, benefit statements) retained by Gamma and available to ER GmbH upon request.

### 3.2 Service Markup

A markup of **10% (ten percent)** is applied to the total cost base for each representative. This markup compensates Gamma for:

- Acting as legal employer and bearing associated employment liability.
- Administering payroll, HR, and compliance obligations under UAE law.
- Assuming regulatory and statutory risk.
- Providing management oversight and operational infrastructure.

The 10% markup is the established internal policy for this relationship and is consistent with the arm's length range for routine service and employer-of-record arrangements (see Transfer Pricing Documentation ER-TP-001).

### 3.3 Formula

```
Monthly Recharge Amount = (Total Cost per Representative) × 1.10
```

**Example (using current agreed rate):**

| Line | Amount (AED) |
|---|---|
| Underlying cost per representative | ~5,227 |
| 10% markup | ~523 |
| **Monthly invoice to ER GmbH** | **5,750** |

The monthly fee is currently fixed at AED 5,750 per representative per month, as agreed in the Sales Support and Integration Partner Agreement (01.03.2026, §4). This fixed amount shall be reviewed and updated whenever the underlying cost base changes materially (defined as a variance of more than 5% from the cost base supporting the agreed fixed fee).

---

## 4. Invoicing Procedures

### 4.1 Invoice Issuance

Gamma shall issue a monthly invoice to ER GmbH for each assigned representative within the first 5 business days of the following calendar month, covering the prior month's services.

### 4.2 Invoice Content

Each invoice must include:

- Invoice date and unique invoice number
- Reference to the Sales Support and Integration Partner Agreement
- Name(s) or reference code(s) of the assigned representative(s)
- Cost base per representative (or aggregate if multiple representatives)
- 10% markup calculation
- Total amount due in AED
- Gamma's bank account details
- Payment due date (net 15 days from invoice date)

### 4.3 Supporting Documentation

Gamma shall, on a quarterly basis or upon ER GmbH's written request, provide a cost breakdown confirming that the invoiced amounts are consistent with the cost-plus-10% methodology. Supporting documents may include payroll summaries, benefit cost schedules, and overhead allocation statements.

### 4.4 Payment

ER GmbH shall pay each invoice within **15 (fifteen) calendar days** of receipt. Payments shall be made in AED to Gamma's designated bank account unless otherwise agreed.

### 4.5 Currency and Accounting

ER GmbH records the expense in EUR, converted at the exchange rate on the invoice date. Foreign exchange differences are treated as financial items in ER GmbH's profit and loss account.

---

## 5. Adjustments and Updates

### 5.1 Change in Number of Representatives

The number of assigned representatives may be increased or reduced by ER GmbH with 30 days' written notice to Gamma. Invoices shall reflect the actual number of representatives assigned in each billing month on a pro-rata basis where applicable.

### 5.2 Annual Review of Fixed Fee

The parties shall review the fixed monthly fee (currently AED 5,750/representative) annually, no later than the end of February each year, to confirm alignment with underlying costs and the 10% markup policy. Any agreed changes shall be documented in a written amendment or side letter to the agreement.

### 5.3 Material Cost Changes

If underlying personnel costs change materially during the year (e.g., salary increase, new benefit entitlement, change in UAE statutory obligations), Gamma shall notify ER GmbH in writing within 30 days, and the parties shall agree an updated fee consistent with the cost-plus-10% formula.

---

## 6. Record Keeping

Both parties are required to maintain the following records for a minimum of **10 years** (consistent with German tax record retention requirements under §147 AO):

- All invoices issued and received under this policy
- Cost documentation supporting each invoice (payroll records, benefit statements, overhead schedules)
- Bank statements evidencing payment
- Correspondence regarding fee adjustments
- This policy document and any amendments

ER GmbH shall maintain a file of all Gamma invoices as part of its transfer pricing documentation package.

---

## 7. Compliance and Governance

**Transfer Pricing Compliance.** This policy is designed to ensure compliance with §1 AStG (Germany) and the OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines. It should be read in conjunction with Transfer Pricing Documentation ER-TP-001 and the Business Relationship Description document.

**Tax Reporting.** ER GmbH shall declare all payments under this policy in its German corporate tax return and maintain this policy as part of its related-party transaction documentation. Gamma shall comply with all applicable UAE tax reporting requirements.

**Internal Audit.** The Finance function of ER GmbH shall review compliance with this policy at least annually, including verification that invoiced amounts match the agreed formula and that supporting documentation is available.

**Policy Amendments.** This policy may only be amended by written agreement signed by authorized representatives of both ER GmbH and Gamma.
