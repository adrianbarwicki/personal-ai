## Question

Where does the CEO assistant n8n workflow deliver the biggest personal gain for Adrian — in terms of time saved, decision quality, or cognitive load reduced?

## Why it matters

The workflow is live (or nearly live) but its full potential isn't obvious yet. Knowing which use patterns produce the most value will shape how Adrian actually uses it day-to-day, what categories or templates to expand, and whether to invest further in the automation layer. Without this, the tool risks being underused or misused.

## How to research

- [ ] Log every message sent through the assistant for 2 weeks; tag each by category and note whether the output was immediately useful, needed editing, or was discarded
- [ ] Review the filed artifacts after 2 weeks: which categories produced artefacts that actually got acted on? Which just accumulated?
- [ ] Estimate time-per-thought-captured before vs after (rough: how long did it take to write up an idea / task / backlog card manually?)
- [ ] Interview yourself: which moments in the day does the assistant remove the most friction? (context-switching into Notion/GitHub, writing JIRA cards, capturing ideas during calls)
- [ ] Compare categories: execution-tasks likely have fast ROI (low effort, clear done state); engineering-backlog cards have high leverage (remove back-and-forth with devs); ceo-ideas may have the slowest ROI but protect against lost insights
- [ ] Check if there are whole workflows not yet covered — e.g. meeting prep, weekly review, OKR updates — that could become new message types
