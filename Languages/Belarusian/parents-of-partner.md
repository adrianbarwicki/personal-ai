# Belarusian — Meeting Lia's Parents

Use selectively. Russian is the safe default — but dropping Belarusian phrases signals **awareness of identity**, which matters especially for families that left Belarus post-2020 or post-2022.

> **Ask Lia first:** "What do your parents speak at home — Russian, Belarusian, or trasianka (mix)?" Her answer tells you everything about the family's politics and identity.

---

## When to use Belarusian vs. Russian

| Family profile | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Older urban, stayed in Belarus | Russian (often exclusively) |
| Rural / western Belarus | Belarusian phrases will mean a lot |
| Diaspora post-2020 / post-2022 | **Strongly prefer Belarusian** — Russian can land badly |
| Soviet-nostalgic, pro-regime | Stick to Russian |

**Hybrid strategy:** lead with Russian, drop **one Belarusian word** (e.g. *Дзякуй!* instead of *Спасибо!*) and watch reaction. If they warm up, do more. If neutral, stay in Russian.

---

## Three sounds that mark Belarusian as separate

These signal you noticed Belarusian is its own language, not "Russian dialect":

1. **Дз** (as in *Дзякуй*) — like English *dz* in "adze". Not hard *d*. Practice: *dzya-dzya-dzya*.
2. **Ў** (as in *чуў*, *усё*) — like English *w* in "wow." Not a vowel, not a *v*. Unique to Belarusian.
3. **Ы** (as in *вы*) — like *i* in "bit," deeper in throat. Not *ee*.

## When you don't understand

| Belarusian | Pronunciation | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Прабачце, не разумею** | *Prabáchtse, ne razuméyu* | Sorry, I don't understand |
| **Паўтарыце, калі ласка** | *Pautarýtse, kalí láska* | Please repeat |
| **Павольней, калі ласка** | *Pavól'ney, kalí láska* | Slower, please |

**Калі ласка** (*kalí láska*) = "please" — sprinkle everywhere.

## Killer combo — full goodbye

> **Дзякуй за цёплы прыём. Было вельмі смачна. Беражыце сябе, да пабачэння!**
>
> *Dzyákuj za tsyópli pryyóm. Býlo vyél'mi smáchna. Berazhýtse syabé, da pabachénnya!*
>
> "Thanks for the warm welcome. It was very tasty. Take care, goodbye!"

## The single-phrase test

If you're going to use **one** Belarusian word in an otherwise Russian conversation, make it:

> **Дзякуй** *(Dzyákuj)* — "Thanks"

Drop it at the end of the meal, looking at the mother. If she lights up — they're a Belarusian-identity family. Expand from there.
