# Russian — Meeting Lia's Parents

Practical phrasebook for meeting and being around Lia's parents (Belarusian, Russian-speaking at home). Organized by moment in a visit.

> **Before you go:** ask Lia for both parents' **patronymics** (отчество). Using them on first contact is the single highest-leverage move. See "Patronymics" below.

---

## Patronymics — the secret weapon

Formed from the father's first name:

- Male endings: **-ович / -евич** (Иванович, Сергеевич)
- Female endings: **-овна / -евна** (Ивановна, Сергеевна)

Examples:
- Dad **Александр**, his father **Иван** → call him **Александр Иванович** (*Aleksándr Ivánovich*)
- Mom **Татьяна**, her father **Сергей** → call her **Татьяна Сергеевна** (*Tatyána Sergyéyevna*)

Use first-name + patronymic on first contact until they explicitly say *"называйте меня просто [first name]"* or *"давайте на ты"*.

---

## ты vs. вы — the formality system

| Form | Use for |
|------|---------|
| **ты** (*ty*) | Friends, peers, kids — **never with parents until invited** |
| **вы** (*vy*) | Elders, strangers, parents, anyone you respect |

The invitation to switch sounds like:
- **"Давайте на ты"** — *"Davájte na ty"* — "Let's switch to ты"
- **"Называйте меня просто Саша"** — drops patronymic + implies ты

Acknowledge immediately when offered: **"Хорошо, спасибо!"** (*Khorashó, spasíba!*) and switch from that moment on. Mom may invite ты before dad — treat as separate decisions.

## 1. Arrival — first 30 seconds

| Russian | Pronunciation | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Здравствуйте!** | *Zdrávstvujte!* | Hello (formal) |
| **Добрый день!** | *Dóbryj dyen'!* | Good day |
| **Добрый вечер!** | *Dóbryj vécher!* | Good evening |
| **Очень приятно познакомиться** | *Óchin' priyátna paznakómitsa* | Very nice to meet you |
| **Я много о вас слышал** | *Ya mnóga o vas slýshal* | I've heard a lot about you (male speaker) |

Handshake with dad, follow mom's lead. Don't initiate cheek kisses.

## 2. Saying who you are

| Russian | Pronunciation | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Меня зовут Адриан** | *Menyá zovút Adrián* | My name is Adrian |
| **Я из Польши** | *Ya iz Pól'shi* | I'm from Poland |
| **Я работаю в [сфере]** | *Ya rabótayu v [sfére]* | I work in [field] |
| **Я учу русский** | *Ya uchú rússkij* | I'm learning Russian |
| **Извините, я ещё плохо говорю** | *Izviníte, ya yeshchó plókha govoryú* | Sorry, I still speak poorly |

The last line is the **humility move** — disarms expectations and earns goodwill.

## 3. At the table — food is the love language

| Russian | Pronunciation | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Приятного аппетита!** | *Priyátnogo appetíta!* | Bon appétit |
| **Спасибо, с удовольствием** | *Spasíba, s udovól'stviyem* | Thanks, with pleasure (accepting food) |
| **Очень вкусно!** | *Óchin' vkúsna!* | Very tasty! |
| **Я давно такого не ел** | *Ya davnó takóva ne yel* | I haven't had this in a long time |
| **Можно ещё немного?** | *Mózhna yeshchó nemnóga?* | May I have a little more? |
| **Спасибо, я наелся** | *Spasíba, ya nayélsya* | Thanks, I'm full |
| **Всё было прекрасно** | *Vsyo býlo prekrásna* | Everything was wonderful |

**Rules:**
- Take seconds the first time you're offered. Refusing reads as "I didn't enjoy it."
- Take small portions if you must, but accept.
- Praise the food. Multiple times. Especially looking at the mother.

## 6. Receiving compliments — deflect, don't accept

The Russian convention is humility, not gratitude.

- ❌ Western: "Thank you, I appreciate that."
- ✅ **"Ой, что вы, я ещё учусь"** (*Oj, shto vy, ya yeshchó uchús'*) — "Oh come on, I'm still learning."
- ✅ Best: **"Спасибо, но это всё Лия — она хорошая учительница"** — "Thanks, but it's all Lia — she's a good teacher."

## 7. When you don't understand

| Russian | Pronunciation | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Извините, не понимаю** | *Izviníte, ne ponimáyu* | Sorry, I don't understand |
| **Повторите, пожалуйста** | *Povtoríte, pozhálujsta* | Please repeat |
| **Помедленнее, пожалуйста** | *Pomédlenneye, pozhálujsta* | Slower, please |
| **Как это по-английски?** | *Kak éta po-anglíjski?* | How do you say that in English? |
| **Лия, помоги, пожалуйста** | *Líya, pomogí, pozhálujsta* | Lia, help me please |

**Пожалуйста** (*pozhálujsta*) = please / you're welcome. Sprinkle everywhere.

## 8. Toasts — drinking has structure

If they pour cognac/vodka/samogon, there's a ritual. **Never refuse the first shot** — sip if you must.

**Order:**
1. **First toast** — host's (dad's). Often *"За знакомство"* (*Za znakómstvo* — "to our acquaintance"). Don't toast over him.
2. **Second toast** — traditionally *"за родителей"* or *"за здоровье"*. You can volunteer here.
3. **Third toast** is sacred: **"За тех, кого нет с нами"** (*Za tekh, kavó nyet s námi*) — "to those no longer with us." **Stand up. Drink in silence. Do not clink glasses.** Then sit.

**Your toast to volunteer:**
> **"За ваше здоровье и за то, что вы воспитали такую дочку."**
> *Za váshe zdaróvye i za to, shto vy vospitáli takúyu dóchku.*
> "To your health, and to the fact that you raised such a daughter."

Use once. It works.

**Drinking rules:**
- Don't pour your own vodka first — pour for the dad, he'll pour yours.
- Don't put a half-full vodka glass back on the table.
- Empty bottles go under the table, not on it.
- If declining: **"Я за рулём"** (*Ya za rulyóm* — "I'm driving") or **"Я не пью"** (*Ya ne p'yu* — "I don't drink").

## 9. The goodbye sequence (10 stages, ~30 min total)

### Stage 1 — Signal you're winding down (still seated)
- **"Нам, наверное, пора собираться"** — "We should probably start getting ready"
- **"Так хорошо у вас, не хочется уходить"** — "It's so nice here, we don't want to leave"

### Stage 2 — Standing up, thanking the meal
- **"Спасибо большое за угощение"** — "Thank you so much for the food"
- **"Татьяна Сергеевна, у вас золотые руки"** — power compliment

### Stage 3 — Moving toward the hallway
- **"Ещё раз спасибо за тёплый приём"** — "Once again, thanks for the warm welcome"
- **"Чувствуется, что я в семье"** — "I feel like I'm among family" (use once, when you mean it)

### Stage 4 — Putting on coats (the longest stage)
- **"Обязательно приедем ещё"** — "We'll definitely come again"
- **"Приезжайте к нам в гости"** — "Come visit us" (return invitation matters)
- **"Будем держать связь"** — "Let's stay in touch"

### Stage 5 — Wishes for them
- **"Берегите себя"** — "Take care of yourselves"
- **"Будьте здоровы"** — "Be healthy"
- **"Всего вам самого доброго"** — "All the best to you"
- **"Передавайте привет [name]"** — "Say hi to [grandma, sibling]"

### Stage 6 — Travel wishes (if anyone's heading out)
- **"Счастливого пути"** — "Happy journey"
- **"Лёгкой дороги"** — "Easy road"

### Stage 7 — The goodbye word itself
- **"До свидания!"** — standard, always safe
- **"До скорой встречи!"** — "See you soon" (warmer)
- ❌ **Не "пока"** — too casual for parents
- ❌ **Не "прощайте"** — means "farewell forever," sounds tragic

### Stage 8 — Threshold rules
- Don't shake hands across the threshold.
- Don't hug across the threshold.
- Don't say goodbye standing in the doorway.

### Stage 9 — Final words at the door
- **"Ещё раз огромное спасибо"** — "Once again, enormous thanks"
- **"Не болейте"** — "Don't get sick"

### Stage 10 — Turn back and wave
After you're outside, turn around once and wave. They're watching from the door or window.

## 10. Three full goodbye scripts

### Script A — Standard warm (first meeting)
> **Спасибо вам огромное за гостеприимство. Всё было прекрасно — и еда, и вечер. Очень приятно было познакомиться. Берегите себя, передавайте привет [name]. До свидания!**

### Script B — Warmer (you connected during the visit)
> **Татьяна Сергеевна, Александр Иванович — спасибо за тёплый приём. Чувствуется, что я в семье. У вас золотые руки, Татьяна Сергеевна — я давно так вкусно не ел. Обязательно приедем ещё. Берегите себя, и пусть у вас всё будет хорошо. До скорой встречи!**

### Script C — Composite
> **Спасибо за вкусную еду и за то, что приняли меня как родного. Чувствуется, что я в семье.**
>
> "Thanks for the delicious food and for receiving me like family. I feel like I'm among family."

## 12. Pronunciation pitfalls

1. **ы** (as in *вы*, *было*) — deep *i*, not "ee". Practice: *ty, vy, my, bylo*.
2. **щ** (as in *пожалуйста*, *ещё*) — soft *shch*. Practice: *yeshchó*.
3. **р** — rolled, single tap. Never English *r*.
4. **Soft consonants** (*ть, дь, нь, ль*) — like there's a tiny *y* glued on. *Dyen'* not *den*.
5. **Stress matters more than sound.** *Спасибо* is *spa-**sí**-ba*, not *spa-si-**bo***.

---

## 13. What NOT to say

- **Нет** alone is curt. Soften: **К сожалению, нет** (*K sazhaléniyu, nyet*).
- **Пока** — too casual for parents.
- **Ты** — never until invited.
- No politics — Belarus / Russia / Ukraine / Lukashenko / 2020 protests.
- No comments on accent, "where you're really from," or family in Belarus.
- Don't compliment Lia's looks in front of them — compliment her **character**: *умная, добрая, заботливая* (smart, kind, caring).

## 14. Next-day follow-up text

Send this in Russian, even if broken — closes the loop and separates "polite guest" from "future son-in-law":

> **Спасибо вам ещё раз за вчерашний вечер. Было очень тепло и уютно. Берегите себя!**
>
> *Spasíba vam yeshchó raz za vcheráshniy vyécher. Býlo óchin' teplo i uyútna. Berregíte sibyá!*
>
> "Thanks again for last night. It was very warm and cozy. Take care!"
